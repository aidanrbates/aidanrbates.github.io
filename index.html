ABOUT

The Ethics in Action conference wishes to generate a space within which participants can engage with the bridge between ethics and action, theory and practice. These two ideas have often been separated, where ethics remains in the world of academics, and action has belonged to activists. This conference seeks to reclaim ethics, and place it within its active domain of ethical action, where theory and practice no longer remain isolated, but naturally evolve into praxis. The Ethics in Action conference is a networking ground for inspiration, bringing curious and critical minds together who are interested in bridging the gap, or who are already engaging in such praxis.

The conference will consist of a keynote speaker, symposium, and breakout sessions which will include workshops and seminars. Students, faculty, grassroots activists and community members are invited to present on their particular ethical inquiries and practices. Participants will have access to both ethical theory and insight into the ethos of activism and organizing. We all have something to learn from one another, and this conference seeks to create that learning space!

Ethics begs the question of how to live, action generates the movement to create a different tomorrow. We hope that our participants can narrow towards a realization of this deeply personal question of how to live. And furthermore, we hope that through collaboration and discourse, participants may feel empowered as ethical agents to act collaboratively in order create the world they wish to live in.

SYMPOSIUM

The symposium is an opportunity for students, faculty and community members to present papers and powerpoint presentations aligned with the theme of ethics in action. The symposium provides a space where participants from across disciplines may share how ethical action arises in their field of inquiry and work.

WORKSHOPS

Workshops provide an opportunity for participants to present their ethical practices interactively within a group of fellow conference participants. Workshops offer ways to engage participants in practices that may be in line with work a facilitator or organization has been working on outside of the conference.

SEMINARS

Participants can propose to hold and facilitate seminars that surround a certain text, video, or question related to the inquiry of ethical action.

TABLING

Involved in an organization that wishes to attend the conference? We invite organizations to table as a way to network and share the work they have been doing in the community.

KEYNOTE

Sarah Alisabeth Fox

Environmental Historian

Sarah is an Evergreen Alum of ‘02, and is currently getting her PhD at the University of British Columbia in History. Sarah is an environmental historian with a focus in folklore, and has spent over the past 10 years recording the lived experiences of Downwinders, those who live in the tailwinds of nuclear testing. Her book Downwind: A People’s History of the Nuclear West focuses on the lived experiences of those who are impacted by radiological exposure in the Great Basin. Her work does the stunning act of turning folk into fact, and gives voice to those who have gone long unlistened to.

PRE-SEMINARS

Pre-seminars are an opportunity for students, faculty, and community to share space and participate in engaging seminar discussions based on chosen texts that will lead us deeper into our inquiry of ethical action. Pre-seminars will take place on 3 dates prior to the conference, and are a space to form a community of thinkers, within which inspiration and collaboration for the conference may take place. All are welcome to attend the pre-seminars, and we ask that if you are unable to do the readings, feel free to still attend with an open mind that discussion may primarily be focused on chosen texts.

HOW DO YOU CREATE THE WORLD YOU WANT TO LIVE IN?

Together, we can pull ethics out of abstraction, find examples of how to act ethically, and attempt to answer our central question: how do I create the world I wish to live in? In the pre-seminars, we will challenge ourselves to address this question by drawing on past and present work to create projects that will shape our future. During the conference, we will gather in a group symposium, small group workshops, seminars and displays of art and informative posters.

All sessions will provide opportunities to recast human beings as subjects, rather than objects venture across disciplinary lines to invoke deeper thinking, to explore unfamiliar paradigms, and to create mindful dialogue that is oriented toward inspired action.

SYMPOSIUM PRESENTATION OF AN ESSAY

Time Alloted: 20 mins

Essay presentations are an opportunity to share with the community writing one has been doing in a program, in their own personal research, or to challenge one to give words to the actions and thoughts that have been occuring. Presentations of essays will consist of the presenter reading all or a selected portion of their essay. Presentations will be grouped and have a panel Q&A at the end of the session.

In order to present an essay, one must submit a proposal that includes:
  • A 250 - 500 word abstract that clearly states thesis, objectives, and lines of inquiry
  • Essay title & list of research resources (titles & author)
  • Short presenter bio
  • Logistical needs

SYMPOSIUM PRESENTATION

Time Alloted: 20 mins

Presentations may consist of powerpoints, videos, or other forms of presentations that are aimed to share with the audience current or past projects and ideas. Presentations will be grouped and have a panel Q&A at the end of the session.

In order to present a presentation, one must submit a proposal that includes:
  • A 250 - 500 word abstract of presentation that states objectives, and lines of inquiry
  • Title & list of research resources (titles & author)
  • Short presenter bio
  • Logistical needs
  • Organization mission statement (if applicable)

WORKSHOP PROPOSAL

Time Alloted: 40 mins

Workshops will occur during breakout sessions, and are an opportunity for facilitators to create interactive activities for participants that will promote discussion and generate thought.

In order to facilitate a workshop, one must submit a proposal that includes:
  • A 250 - 500 word abstract including outline for workshop
  • Title & list of research resources (titles & author)
  • Short presenter bio
  • Workshop information (name, project, etc)
  • Organization mission statement (if applicable)
  • Logistical needs
  • Ideal number of participants

SEMINAR PROPOSAL

Time Alloted: 40 mins

Seminars will occur during breakout session, and are an opportunity to have in depth discussions engaging in critical questions that surround the theme of the conference.

In order to facilitate a seminar, one must submit a proposal that includes:
  • Two page outline of activities and/or readings
  • Seminar title
  • Seminar information (name, project, etc)
  • Organization mission statement (if applicable)
  • Learning outcomes
  • Logistical needs
  • Intended audience
  • Ideal number of participants

POSTERS/ARTWORK PROPOSAL

Posters and Artwork will be displayed in public places for all attendees. This is an opportunity to promote different projects currently in the works or giving artistic and creative expression to thoughts and ideas that surround the theme of the conference.

In order to display a poster or art, one must submit a proposal that includes:
  • 300-word abstract including type of project or form of art (Size), learning objectives, intended audience and effects, and how your project relates to central themes of conference
  • Title and list of research resources (titles and authors)
  • Short Presenter Bio
  • Organization mission statement (if applicable)
  • Learning outcomes
  • Logistical needs
